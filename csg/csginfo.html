CSG, the Crypto Simulation Group, is a very small group of like-minded historian/researchers in the field of cryptology. The six members are actively engaged in the studies of cipher machines, cryptographic procedures and cryptanalytical methods used by the various participants during the Second World War.

The members' expertise is varied and extensive as well as being quite complementary, which allows us to take on a variety of rather complex projects such as the simulation of cipher machines and cryptanalytical machinery. In particular, we have invested a lot of effort in the simulation of the various models of the Enigma.

You can get a glimpse of some of our simulation activites by visiting our gallery. CSG Gallery (graphic intensive).
